CRAAM

CRAAM is a research center operated through an agreement between the Mackenzie Presbyterian University and the MCT/INPE - National Institute for Space Research. Its main objectives include:

  • High-quality technical and scientific research in the areas of Radio Sciences, Solar Physics, Solar Flares, Solar-Terrestrial Relations, Space Weather, Earth's Atmosphere, Space Geodesy, Stellar Activity and Exoplanets, Instrumentation, and Terahertz Technology.
  • Operation of experimental resources, including the Submillimetric Solar Telescope (SST), Solar Radio Telescopes in Millimeter Waves (POEMAS), Very Low-Frequency Tracking Stations (Brazil, Peru, Argentina, and Mexico), Solar-Terrestrial Terahertz Experiment SOLAR-T (with GRIPS, U.C. Berkeley), Solar Telescope at 30THz and Visible (El Leoncito and São Paulo), Space Radio Observatory in the Northeast (Eusébio, CE), Itapetinga Radio Observatory (Atibaia, SP), Cosmic Particle Detectors, Sensors for Solar and Atmospheric Neutron Detection, X-ray and Gamma-ray Spectrometers, Experiments in Antarctica, Earth's Electric Field.
  • Support for the Postgraduate Program in Geospatial Sciences and Applications (CAGE) at UPM/EE. Support for education and training at the levels of scientific initiation, master's, doctoral, and postdoctoral interns.

For more information, visit the CRAAM website.
